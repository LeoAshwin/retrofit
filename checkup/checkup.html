Basic Self-Checkups

Fitness Self-Checkups

1. Resting Heart Rate (RHR): Measure your pulse when at rest. Lower RHR indicates better cardiovascular fitness.

2. Step Test: Step up and down on a bench for 3 minutes and check your heart rate to assess endurance.

3. Flexibility Test: Touch your toes or do a sit-and-reach test to check joint flexibility.

4. Push-Up Test: Count how many push-ups you can do in 1 minute to evaluate upper body strength.

5. Single-Leg Stand: Test your balance by standing on one leg with your eyes closed.

6. Waist Circumference: Measure your waist at the navel to check for health risks associated with excess abdominal fat.

Diet Self-Checkups

1. Hydration Levels: Monitor urine color (light yellow is optimal) to assess hydration.

2. Daily Calorie Intake: Use a food diary or app to track your daily intake for weight management.

3. Protein Intake: Track protein to ensure you’re meeting fitness goals. Aim for 0.8-1.2g/kg body weight.

4. Portion Control: Use hand portions to control serving sizes (fist for carbs, palm for protein, thumb for fats).

5. Balanced Diet: Ensure you’re consuming foods from all essential food groups.

6. Macro and Micronutrient Check: Track essential nutrients to prevent deficiencies.

7. Body Fat Percentage: Use a body fat caliper or smart scale to monitor body composition.

8. Meal Timing: Track meal frequency to maintain stable energy levels and avoid overeating.
