Breathing Techniques

This simple breathing technique helps to slow down your breathing pace by having you apply deliberate effort in each breath.

Practice using this breath 4 to 5 times a day when you begin so that you can correctly learn the breathing pattern.

  • Relax your neck and shoulders.
  • Keeping your mouth closed, inhale slowly through your nose for 2 counts.
  • Pucker or purse your lips as though you were going to whistle.
  • Exhale slowly by blowing air through your pursed lips for a count of 4.

Diaphragmatic breathing (aka belly breathing) helps you use your diaphragm properly. It can be particularly helpful for people with breathing challenges due to chronic conditions.

Practice diaphragmatic breathing for 5 to 10 minutes 3 to 4 times daily.

  • Lie on your back with your knees slightly bent and your head on a pillow.
  • Place one hand on your upper chest and one hand below your rib cage.
  • Slowly inhale through your nose, feeling your stomach pressing into your hand.
  • Exhale using pursed lips, keeping your upper hand still.
  • Try placing a book on your abdomen to increase difficulty.

This technique uses imagery or focus words and phrases to enhance relaxation.

Start with a 10-minute session and gradually increase the duration to at least 20 minutes.

  • Bring your awareness to your breaths and notice differences between normal and deep breathing.
  • Place one hand below your belly button, and notice how it rises and falls with each breath.
  • Combine deep breathing with a focus word or phrase like "peace" or "relax".

Lion’s breath is an energizing yoga practice that helps relieve tension in your jaw and facial muscles.

  • Sit comfortably with your palms against your knees.
  • Inhale deeply through your nose and open your eyes wide.
  • Open your mouth wide, stick out your tongue, and make a long "haaa" sound as you exhale.

Alternate nostril breathing is a relaxation technique that enhances cardiovascular function and lowers heart rate.

  • Close your right nostril with your thumb and inhale through your left nostril.
  • Close your left nostril with your fingers and exhale through your right nostril.
  • Inhale through your right nostril, close it, and exhale through your left nostril.
  • Continue this pattern for up to 5 minutes.

Equal breathing involves making your inhales and exhales of the same length, helping to bring balance and relaxation.

  • Choose a comfortable seated position.
  • Breathe in and out through your nose, counting the duration of each breath.
  • Practice for at least 5 minutes.

Resonant breathing involves breathing at a rate of 5 full breaths per minute, helping to reduce stress and improve heart rate variability.

  • Inhale for a count of 5.
  • Exhale for a count of 5.
  • Continue this pattern for a few minutes.

Sitali breath helps lower body temperature and relax your mind.

  • Stick out your tongue and curl the outer edges together.
  • Inhale through your mouth.
  • Exhale out through your nose.
  • Continue for up to 5 minutes.

Deep breathing helps to relieve shortness of breath and helps you feel more relaxed and centered.

  • Stand or sit, drawing your elbows back slightly to allow your chest to expand.
  • Inhale deeply through your nose.
  • Retain your breath for a count of 5.
  • Exhale slowly through your nose.

This practice involves making a humming sound to create a calming effect and reduce frustration, anxiety, and anger.

  • Close your eyes and relax your face.
  • Place your fingers on the tragus of your ears.
  • Inhale and make a loud humming sound while keeping your mouth closed.
  • Continue as long as comfortable.
