7-Day South Indian Diet Plan for Weight Loss

Day 1 (1500 Calories)

Early Morning (6:00 a.m.)

  • 1 cup warm water with juice of half a lime and honey

Breakfast (7:00 a.m.)

  • 3 millet idlis + 1 small bowl sambar or 2 egg whites + ½ a small bowl of peanut and coconut chutney + 1 cup green tea

Mid Morning (10:00 a.m.)

  • 1 medium apple or a small bowl of grapes

Lunch (12:30-1:00 p.m.)

  • 2 ragi balls + Vegetable curry + 1 cup rasam + 1 cup buttermilk

Post-Lunch (3:30-4:00 p.m.)

  • 1 cup green tea + 2 Marie biscuits

Dinner (7:00 p.m.)

  • 2 rotis + 1 medium bowl of vegetable curry + 1 cup spinach daal + 1 small bowl of curd

Bed Time (10:00 p.m.)

  • 1 cup warm milk with a pinch of turmeric

Why This Works: This 1500-calorie diet for Day 1 helps boost your energy and lose weight. Warm water with lime and honey kick starts your metabolism, maintains the internal pH, and boosts your immunity. Millet idlis are super healthy, and peanut and coconut chutney will provide your body with healthy fats and protein. Choose sambar or egg whites as a source of lean protein, which will keep you active throughout the day. Green tea helps flush toxins out and keeps your hunger pangs at bay. Apples or grapes are good sources of vitamins and minerals. Ragi balls provide energy, calcium, and fiber. Have any vegetable curry and a cup of rasam to support your body functions. Buttermilk and curd strengthen the digestive system and immunity. Marie biscuits, though not healthy, will help you relax with a cup of green tea. Avoid having white or brown rice for dinner, and consume no more than two rotis. You can have one extra cup of daal if you feel hungry. Drinking warm milk with turmeric will prevent you from snacking on junk and help you sleep better.

Day 1 Exercise Plan

  • Warm-up – 10 mins
  • Rope jumping – 3 sets of 50 reps
  • Crunches – 2 sets of 15 reps
  • Bicycle crunches – 2 sets of 15 reps
  • Leg-up crunches – 2 sets of 15 reps
  • Sit-ups – 2 sets of 15 reps
  • Russian twist – 2 sets of 25 reps
  • Flutter kicks – 2 sets of 30 reps
  • Scissor legs – 2 sets of 15 reps
  • Leg raises – 2 sets of 15 reps
  • Brisk walk – 10 minutes
  • Cool off by doing some stretching exercises

How You Will Feel By The End Of Day 1: By the end of Day 1, you will feel refreshed and active. Adhering to the diet plan will not be a problem as you will be consuming healthier versions of familiar foods in adequate amounts. If you exercise after a long time, you will experience body pain, and that is why you need to warm up and cool off properly before and after exercising. Make sure you don’t overdo the exercises. Check with your doctor to know if you are allowed to do vigorous cardio or weight training before you start exercising.

Day 2 (1400 Calories)

Early Morning (6:00 a.m.)

  • 1 cup warm water with juice of half a lime and honey

Breakfast (7:00 a.m.)

  • 2 medium rava dosas + tomato and onion chutney + 1 cup black coffee + 4 almonds

Mid Morning (10:00 a.m.)

  • 1 cup cut fruits

Lunch (12:30-1:00 p.m.)

  • 1 small bowl brown rice (or) 2 rotis + 1 cup bendekai gojju + 1 cup sambar + cucumber and carrot salad + 1 cup buttermilk

Post-Lunch (3:30-4:00 p.m.)

  • 1 cup black coffee + 1 boiled egg (or) boiled peanuts (20 g)

Dinner (7:00 p.m.)

  • 2 rotis + 1 cup mixed daal + 1 medium bowl vegetable kadalai curry or chicken curry + cucumber, onion, and tomato salad + 1 small cup low-fat curd

Bed Time (10:00 p.m.)

  • 1 cup warm milk with a pinch of turmeric

Why This Works: Honey detoxifies your system and boosts your immunity. Rava dosa is crispy and healthy. Use olive oil or clarified butter to make the dosa. Almonds are loaded with vitamin E. Black coffee will improve your metabolism, and a cup of cut fruits will fulfill your hunger and provide energy. Brown rice is a healthy alternative to white rice and is high in fiber. Bendekai gojju (okra) is rich in vitamins and minerals. Include cucumber and carrot salad to stay hydrated and keep the digestive system healthy. Boiled peanuts are rich in proteins, while eggs are rich in vitamins. Having mixed daal or chicken curry will keep you full and boost your metabolism. Curd will aid digestion.

Day 2 Exercise Plan

  • Warm-up – 10 mins
  • Jumping jacks – 3 sets of 30 reps
  • Burpees – 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Push-ups – 2 sets of 10 reps
  • Mountain climbers – 3 sets of 15 reps
  • High knees – 2 sets of 30 reps
  • Squats – 3 sets of 15 reps
  • Lunges – 2 sets of 15 reps (each leg)
  • Cool off with stretching exercises

How You Will Feel By The End Of Day 2: Day 2 will be a bit more challenging, especially if you’re not used to doing exercises. You will feel lighter and more energetic, but if you overdo it, you may feel tired. You will need to stay hydrated and keep your meals balanced. Focus on staying consistent with both your diet and exercise to see better results.

Day 3 (1600 Calories)

Early Morning (6:00 a.m.)

  • 1 cup warm water with juice of half a lime and honey

Breakfast (7:00 a.m.)

  • 1 bowl of upma + 1 small bowl of coconut chutney + 1 cup green tea

Mid Morning (10:00 a.m.)

  • 1 cup papaya or a small bowl of mixed fruit

Lunch (12:30-1:00 p.m.)

  • 2 small bowls of vegetable curry + 1 cup brown rice + 1 cup sambar + 1 cup buttermilk

Post-Lunch (3:30-4:00 p.m.)

  • 1 cup black coffee + 1 small bowl of roasted chana

Dinner (7:00 p.m.)

  • 1 bowl of mixed vegetable soup + 1 medium bowl of vegetable salad + 1 small bowl of paneer or tofu curry

Bed Time (10:00 p.m.)

  • 1 cup warm milk with a pinch of turmeric

Why This Works: Lime and honey are great for detoxification. Upma is a wholesome meal that provides carbohydrates and fiber. Coconut chutney provides healthy fats. Papaya is rich in vitamins and aids digestion. Vegetable curry and brown rice provide fiber and essential nutrients. Roasted chana is high in protein. Vegetable soup and salad are low in calories and high in nutrients, while paneer or tofu provide protein.

Day 3 Exercise Plan

  • Warm-up – 10 mins
  • Jogging or brisk walk – 20 mins
  • Push-ups – 3 sets of 15 reps
  • Squats – 3 sets of 20 reps
  • Planks – 2 sets of 1 minute
  • Burpees – 3 sets of 12 reps
  • Cool off with stretching exercises

How You Will Feel By The End Of Day 3: By Day 3, you will feel your energy levels rising and your body getting accustomed to the exercise routine. You may feel hungrier, but the balanced meals and exercise will help maintain your weight loss goals. Ensure you stay hydrated and keep up with the exercise routine.
